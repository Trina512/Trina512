// Golf League //

Cross-platform app

My client would like to have a cross-platform app to help his customers to check out all golf players stats wherever and whenever. Apart from that, he needed a feature built in the app to make a profit.

Home Page

What's the problem?

For this project, I confronted two major problems.

1: the best way to embark it was to build a responsive design for different devices, but it was quite challenging to arrange loads of players stats appropriately in mobile devices due to the limited screen space.

2: the client had some thoughts of exploiting the app to increase his revenue, but we had to narrow them down and found out a suitable way.

Charts were not ideal for this case, neither tables as scaling them down would make the text and numbers difficult to read or mess the layout.

Giving thought to the design and development aspects, the module layout might be the best way to put on my design. For priority information, I presented them straightly within the sight of the users whereas less important data were folded up. Users are still able to see all the stats if they click or tap see more button. As the result, my design made sure that in which device, the design would keep its consistency and context could be read clearly.

// Solutions //

We did some research on the client’s competitors and current industrial trends, and found out that there were two feasible approaches, a streaming media and an online golf mall.

Both need a great amount of initial investment. Although Internet videos and webcasts of live events need more money due to the copyrights for broadcasting, the return is considerable because of less competition in the industry and evergrowing golf fans. On the other hand, online shopping has harsh competition, and newbies are hard to share the market, despite the less money needed to be put in the first place.

In my opinion, I thought that we could take both into account, so I told him that we could complete one feature before the deadline, then added another one in the next iteration. He agreed and chose streaming media as the first function to be built.

// Sennheiser //

The future of Audio

Three months ago, I worked on a lite app for Sennheiser, which is a well-known brand and represents the advanced audio technology.

Based on client's requirements and target users, this app needed to be designed simply and modern. The app possesses only three sessions, headphones, news and contact. Sennheiser mainly sold headphones here which could be divided into 9 categories, so in headphones, I listed all types and ranked them by popularity. News was where Sennheiser PR department could promote their images and advertise new release products whereas contact provided repair centers' addresses and contact numbers.

I made the user flow clear and simple, utilized the brand colour as primary one in my design, set background black and left the large white space. All of these enabled this mobile app to look technological and up to date.

// Rockland //

Luggage & Travel Accessories

Sorry, your browser does not support the canvas tag.

Rockland

Since watched How to design user interfaces with the Golden Canon Grid (NOT GOLDEN RATION) from Youtube, trying it on my design was planted in my mind.

Not all the apps fit this method, except for some fashionable brands, such as Rockland. Redesigning this app provided me such great opportunity to experiment with it. With the auxiliary lines, I was able to arrange images, shapes and texts to different positions, and consequently got various layouts. I quite enjoyed the whole process.

After several iterations, it left me not much space to improve its user flow, however, I managed to make it more concise. For example, cart and checkout were separated in previous version, which means that users modified quantity in the cart, then go back to make payment. Why wouldn’t they be integrated into one step? Bringing up questions and solving them is one of the skills that UX/UI designers should equip. I did so. I combined cart and checkout together and saved one step for users.

// V's Kitchen //

Vegetarian's Diet

V’s Kitchen is a newly opened vegan restaurant. How to make it stand out from a crowd of apps that look alike was a focal point in this project. I felt that adding a pinch of motion design would make it more appealing and interesting.

I used Photoshop to build a high fidelity prototype and animated it through After Effect. Taking costs and development realization into account, I didn’t create it complicated. The first part of animation aimed to utilize every inch of screen to the most, while the second part was designed to make the user flow more attractive.

Although UI animation consumes time and labour, it delights users and is worth to do.
